    About Me

    I'm an Electrical Engineering student at UBC, focused on embedded firmware, power electronics, and hardware design. My work spans PCB layout in Altium Designer, motor control algorithms on the ESP32 and STM32, and signal conditioning circuits for precision measurement.

    Before university, I spent 7 years at an electronics repair workshop in Bali — diagnosing component-level failures, redesigning control boards, and retrofitting legacy systems with Bluetooth and updated firmware. That background in hands-on troubleshooting informs how I approach system design today.

    Current focus: discrete H-bridge PCB design, closed-loop BLDC motor control with field-oriented control (FOC), and FreeRTOS firmware architecture on STM32.
